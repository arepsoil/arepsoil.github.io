Coconut Oil Constituents

Coconut oil is composed of the fatty acids, caprylic acid C -8:0 (8%), capric acid, C-10:0,(7%), lauric acid C-12:0, (49%), myristic acid C-14:0(8%), palmitic acid C-16:0 (8%), stearic acid C-18:0 (2%), oleic acid C-18:1 (6%) and 2% of C-18:2 linoleic acid.

  • Fat 99.91 g per 100  
  • Saturated 86.91 g
  • Monounsaturated 8.31 g
  • Polyunsaturated 4.69 g
  • Protein 0 g, Cholesterol 0 g
  • Energy 899.19 Kcal

Facts about Coconut oil

The oil is a rich source of vitamin K and the content of vitamin E.

The high oleic oil content indicates that the oil has a higher content of omega-3 and lower content of omega-6 fatty acids. Oleic acid ensures membrane fluidity that is responsible for hormone response, mineral transport and immunity. It also helps maintain proper brain function and has a significant effect on mood and behaviour.

Replacing saturated fats like butter and ghee with sunflower oil that is full of unsaturated fats, will keep your heart healthy. Sunflower oil has a number of compounds like choline and phenolic acid, which are beneficial for the heart. Also, phytosterols in sunflower oil, a naturally occurring plant sterol found in plants, prevents cholesterol absorption by the body.
